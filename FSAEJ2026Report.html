FSAE-EV Scales Summary - June 2026

Published

Overview

A total of 52 teams were scaled before Design. Of the cars, the lightest was 164.7kg (Univ of Wisconsin - Madison), the mean car weight was 215kg, and the median car was 214.3kg.

If a weight sticker indicates “No Accumulator”, the sticker does not include the battery weight, but the battery weight was added to the “Car Only” total on this site.

Methodology

All vehicles were weighed on Intercomp SW650 RFX scales, with the left front scale location used as common datum for all cars. The surface pad was measured within 0.1 deg of level in X, Y, and corner-to-corner, although exact pad locations for the other 3 scales vary with wheelbase and track. At last calibration (2024-04-03), all scales were certified accurate within 0.1% +/- or 1lb +/- (whichever is greater), and a spot check at site setup confirmed all scales consistent at low end (40lb) and high end (275lb) of range used on site.

Cars are weighed twice - once with a driver in the car for balance percentages (teams may select any driver), once with the driver out (for vehicle only weight). If the driver uses a seat spacer/pad, it is removed for the car only measurement (considered driver gear). Cars are presented in “ready to race” configuration, and photographs of the vehicles can be checked at https://bit.ly/FSAEJ26Scales

Remaining questions can be directed to the event co-captain, Alton Worthington.
